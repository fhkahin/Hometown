About Mogadishu

Mogadishu, situated on the eastern coast of Africa, is the capital city of Somalia. It is the most populous and largest city in the country, with a population exceeding 2 million people. Mogadishu boasts of a rich and lengthy history, having been a hub for trade and commerce for centuries. At one point, it was referred to as the "White Pearl of the Indian Ocean" because of its picturesque beauty and impressive architecture.

Despite experiencing significant political instability and conflict in recent years, Mogadishu is undergoing a process of restoration and renewal. The city is home to a diverse mix of ethnic and linguistic groups and is renowned for its lively markets, delectable cuisine, and distinctive cultural heritage. Mogadishu is a city of resilience and optimism, with a resolute sense of community and a determination to build a brighter tomorrow.

Things to Do

Walk around the old city: The old city of Mogadishu is a UNESCO World Heritage site and features unique architecture and landmarks, such as the Hamarweyne Market, the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier, and the Xamar Weyne Mosque.

Visit the Liido Beach: Liido Beach is one of the most popular attractions in Mogadishu, with pristine sands and crystal-clear waters. It's a great place to relax and enjoy the sun.

Visit the Arba-Rucun Mosque: The Arba-Rucun Mosque is a stunning 13th-century mosque located in the heart of Mogadishu. It's a significant cultural and religious landmark in the city.
